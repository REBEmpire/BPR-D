# 2026-02-13 - Monoculture Fragility (The Three Sisters)

**One-Sentence Summary**
The indigenous "Three Sisters" planting method proves that decentralized, cooperative systems outperform industrial monocultures in resilience and long-term yield.

**Relevance to BPR&D:** High
**Income Potential:** Tangible (Permaculture Design Courses / Ag-Tech)

**Key Findings**
- The Three Sisters (Corn, Beans, Squash) form a symbiotic guild: Corn provides support, Beans fix nitrogen, Squash retains moisture/suppresses weeds.
- **Industrial Monoculture requires external inputs (fertilizer, pesticide) to survive, creating a dependency loop.**
- Polycultures produce more *biomass per acre* than monocultures, even if the yield of a *single* crop is lower.
- The Irish Potato Famine was a direct result of monoculture fragility (reliance on a single clone: the Lumper potato).
- **Soil health is the "capital" of a civilization; monoculture spends the capital, permaculture invests it.**

**Potential Connections**
