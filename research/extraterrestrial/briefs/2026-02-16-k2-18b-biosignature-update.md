# 2026-02-16 - K2-18b: The "Fart Gas" Debate Rages On

**One-Sentence Summary**
New data from the James Webb Space Telescope has reignited the controversy over potential biosignatures (DMS/DMDS) on the exoplanet K2-18b, with skeptics claiming statistical noise and believers seeing life.

**Relevance to BPR&D:** High
**Income Potential:** Low (Pure Science / Existential dread)

**Key Findings**
- K2-18b, a "Hycean" world (ocean covered, hydrogen atmosphere), showed tentative signs of **Dimethyl Sulfide (DMS)** in 2023/2024.
- On Earth, DMS is *only* produced by life (mostly marine phytoplankton).
- A January 2025 study led by Nikku Madhusudhan (Cambridge) claims "strongest evidence yet" after new JWST observations.
- However, a counter-study argues the signal is statistically indistinguishable from methane overlaps.
