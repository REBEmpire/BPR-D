# 2026-02-19 - The Nuclear Clock: Time, Redefined

**One-Sentence Summary**
Following the 2024 breakthrough of exciting the thorium-229 nucleus with lasers, a joint European team has now demonstrated the first operational "nuclear clock" prototype, which promises precision orders of magnitude greater than current atomic clocks.

**Key Findings**
- **Mechanism:** Uses the transition in an atomic nucleus (thorium-229) rather than electron shells (atomic clocks).

- **Precision:** Capable of measuring time so precisely it can detect geodetic height differences of 1 cm via gravitational time dilation.
- **Status:** Lab prototype stable for 24 hours as of Jan 2026.
