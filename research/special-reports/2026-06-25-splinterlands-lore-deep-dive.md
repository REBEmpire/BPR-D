# 2026-06-25 - Splinterlands Lore: Foundation for BPR&D Game Prototypes
**Author:** Jules (BPR&D Intelligence) | **Category:** Gaming / Lore | **Clearance:** Internal Only

## Executive Summary
This report provides the narrative bedrock for our two upcoming prototypes: **Splinterlands Civilization** (HoMM3-style Strategy) and **Slingerlands RPG** (FPS Open World). The Splinterlands universe is not a generic fantasy setting; it is a fractured reality defined by "The Splintering," where mana is a scarce, volatile resource that dictates hierarchy.

We will leverage this fractured cosmology to justify our specific game mechanics: **The Burn** (Permadeath/NFT destruction) and **The Summoning Bond** (1-Summoner/4-Monster limits).

---

## 1. Cosmology: The Splintering
Centuries ago, a cataclysmic event known as **The Splintering** shattered the planet into six distinct "Splinters," separated by a magical void but connected by the flow of Mana.
*   **The Mana Cap:** Summoners cannot channel infinite power. They are limited by their own mana reserves. This is the **canonical justification** for our unit caps (4 monsters) and turn-based resource limits.
*   **The Summoners:** These are not just generals; they are conduits. Without a Summoner, monsters are mindless or trapped in their home planes.

## 3. Game 1 Integration: "Splinterlands Civ" (HoMM3 Style)

### The Mechanic: "The Burn" (Permadeath)
In our Civ game, units are NFTs. If they die, they are burned.
*   **Lore Justification: The Void Transaction.**
    *   To summon a creature into this "High Stakes" arena, the Summoner enters a **Void Pact**.
    *   If the creature's physical form is destroyed in this specific arena, its essence is not returned to the mana pool (as normal). Instead, it is **consumed by the Void** to pay the debt of the Summoner.
    *   **In-Game Text:** *"The Void demands a tithe. Your [Monster Name] has been claimed forever."*

### The Map Strategy
*   **Biomes:** The map should strictly follow the Splinter geography. Crossing from the *Burning Lands* to *Azmare* should incur movement penalties unless the player has "Amphibious" or "Flying" units.
*   **Cities:** Capturing a city (e.g., Ferexia) should grant passive bonuses to that specific Splinter's units (e.g., +1 Melee for Fire units).

## 4. Game 2 Integration: "Slingerlands RPG" (FPS Open World)

### The Mechanic: The Summoning Bond
*   **The Player Character:** You are the **Summoner**. You are physically weak but magically potent.
*   **The 4-Monster Limit:** You can only mentally link with 4 constructs at once.
    *   **Gameplay:** You don't "shoot" enemies; you point and command your *Tank* (e.g., Serpent of Eld) to engage, while you shield your *Ranged* unit (e.g., Spark Pixies).
    *   **Mana Exhaustion:** Summoning high-mana creatures (like a *Kron*) slows your movement speed or dims your vision, simulating the mental strain.

### Quest Opportunities
*   **The Chaos Legion Invasion:** The current era features the invasion of the Chaos Legion (Silas of the Rift). Quests can involve:
    *   *Resistance:* Helping the native Splinters fight off Chaos portals.
    *   *Corruption:* Investigating why the Earth Splinter is turning into a fungal hive-mind (Queen Mycelia's influence).

## 6. Jules' Hot Take & Conclusion
The strength of Splinterlands lore is that it isn't "Good vs. Evil." It's "Order (Life) vs. Freedom (Chaos)" or "Nature (Earth) vs. Industry (Fire)." Even the Death Splinter argues they are the most compassionate faction.

*   **For the Civ Game:** Lean into the *political* friction between these philosophies.
*   **For the RPG:** Lean into the *scale*. Standing next to a **Frost Giant** should feel terrifying.

## 7. Addendum: Open Source Engines & Accelerators
Building from scratch is slow. We should fork and modify existing open-source projects that align with our lore and mechanics.

### For Game 1 (Civ/HoMM3 Strategy):
1.  **Battle for Wesnoth (C++ / Lua)**
    *   **Pros:** The gold standard for open-source turn-based strategy. Massive unit variety, hex-grid combat (perfect for Splinterlands), and a robust campaign editor.
    *   **Cons:** The engine is old (2003) and uses a custom WML scripting language alongside Lua. Harder to modernize visually.
    *   **Fit:** **High.** We can reskin the "Factions" (Loyalists -> Life, Undead -> Death, Orcs -> Fire).
2.  **FreeCiv (C)**
    *   **Pros:** True Civilization clone. Handles city management and tech trees perfectly.
    *   **Cons:** Combat is too abstract (stacks of units vs tactical battles).
    *   **Fit:** **Medium.** Good for the "Empire" layer, bad for the "Card Battle" layer.

### For Game 2 (Slingerlands RPG / FPS):
1.  **Veloren (Rust)**
    *   **Pros:** An open-world RPG engine inspired by Cube World/Zelda. Procedural generation (perfect for "The Splintering" landscapes). Multiplayer-native.
    *   **Cons:** Voxel-based art style (Minecraft-ish). Might not fit the "High Fantasy" card art unless we lean into a "glitch/digital" aesthetic.
    *   **Fit:** **High.** The Rust codebase is modern, performant, and safe.
2.  **Flare (C++)**
    *   **Pros:** Diablo-style Action RPG engine. Great for dungeon crawling and loot.
    *   **Cons:** Isometric view, not First Person.
    *   **Fit:** **Medium.** If we pivot to a top-down view, this is the winner.

### Recommended Path
*   **Fork Battle for Wesnoth** for the Strategy game. It already has the "Hero" and "Level Up" mechanics we need. We just need to inject the "Permadeath/Burn" logic into the unit death handler.
*   **Use Godot 4** for the RPG. While Veloren is great, Godot's GDScript is faster for our AI agents to iterate on. We can grab a "FPS Controller" template and build the "Summoning" mechanics from scratch in a weekend.
