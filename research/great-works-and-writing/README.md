## Scope

The Western canon and the great works that shaped how we think, tell stories, and understand the human condition. Deep literary analysis, author studies, thematic connections, and what these works still teach us.

### Core Works & Authors
- **Alexandre Dumas** — The Count of Monte Cristo, The Three Musketeers
- **Victor Hugo** — Les Misérables, The Hunchback of Notre-Dame
- **J.R.R. Tolkien** — The Lord of the Rings, The Silmarillion, linguistic mythology
- **Patrick Rothfuss** — The Kingkiller Chronicle, narrative craft, magic system design
- **Robert Jordan** — The Wheel of Time, epic scope, world-building
- **The Western Canon** — Homer, Shakespeare, Dante, Milton, Dostoevsky, and beyond

### Areas of Interest
- **Literary Analysis** — Themes, symbolism, narrative structure, character arcs
- **Author Studies** — Lives, influences, creative processes, lesser-known works
- **Craft & Technique** — What makes great writing great? Prose analysis, pacing, world-building
- **Thematic Connections** — Justice (Monte Cristo), redemption (Les Mis), power (Tolkien), knowledge (Rothfuss)
- **Modern Relevance** — What these works say about today's world
- **Cross-Topic Potential** — Norse mythology (Tolkien's sources), history (Hugo's France, Dumas's history), ancient religions (mythological structures in fiction)
