# 2026-02-16 - The Viking Woman and the Scallop Shells

**One-Sentence Summary**
A Viking Age woman's grave in Norway contained two scallop shells placed over her mouth, a practice previously unknown in pre-Christian Scandinavia, baffling experts.

**Relevance to BPR&D:** Medium
**Income Potential:** Low (Cultural trivia / Mystery content)

**Key Findings**
- The grave, dated to the 9th century AD, was found in Trøndelag, Norway.
- The woman was wealthy (oval brooches, jewelry).
- The scallop shells (Pecten maximus) were placed specifically *over the mouth*.
- In later Christian contexts, the scallop is the symbol of St. James (pilgrimage), but this predates widespread Christianity in the region.
- **Tie to BPR&D:** Identifying anomalies in cultural practices that hint at lost knowledge or external contact.
