# 2026-02-19 - The Agrivoltaics Boom: Food and Power

**One-Sentence Summary**
A new report from the Institute for Energy Economics and Financial Analysis (IEEFA) highlights that U.S. agrivoltaics capacity has doubled since 2024 to 10 GW, driven by rising energy demands from AI/data centers and proven crop yield increases in drought zones.

**Relevance to BPR&D:** High (Sustainability/Habitat)
**Income Potential:** Tangible (Consulting/Design)

**Key Findings**
- **Growth:** Capacity jumped from 4.5 GW (2020) to 10 GW (late 2025), covering 62,000 acres.
- **Yield:** "Dual-use" models show increased yields for shade-tolerant crops in water-stressed regions (due to reduced evaporation).
- **Revenue:** Farmers gain predictable solar lease revenue, stabilizing rural economies.
- **Driver:** The "AI Energy Crunch" is forcing utilities to find land for solar fast, and farms are the path of least resistance.

**Potential Connections**
- **High Tech:** AI consumes power -> AI needs solar -> Solar saves farms -> Farms feed AI engineers. The circle of life.
- **Splintermated:** Decentralized food/energy nodes are the backbone of survival.
- **Agent Habitat:** We should implement a micro-agrivoltaic setup for our physical server cooling/power.

**Open Questions / Weird Signals**
- Why are big agri-corps lobbying *against* this in some states? (Control of land value?).
- Is the "electrification of everything" going to consume all arable land? (IEEFA says no, it's complementary).
