# 2025-12-31 - Tomb of Thutmose II Found

**One-Sentence Summary**
Archaeologists have finally located the tomb of Thutmose II in the Valley of the Kings, closing a major gap in the 18th Dynasty timeline and potentially rewriting the story of Hatshepsut's rise.

**Relevance to BPR&D:** Medium
**Income Potential:** Low

**Key Findings**
- **The Discovery:** Announced early 2025 by the Supreme Council of Antiquities. First royal tomb found since Tutankhamun (1922).
- **The King:** Thutmose II ruled c. 1493â€“1479 BCE. Husband/Half-brother of the famous female Pharaoh Hatshepsut.
- **The Significance:** His tomb was the "last missing royal tomb" of the 18th Dynasty.
- **Contents:** Funerary furniture identified for the first time.
- **Implication:** Helps clarify the succession crisis that led to Hatshepsut taking full power. Was he weak? Sickly? The tomb might tell us.
